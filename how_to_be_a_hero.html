How to be a hero?

By piupiupiu September 25, 2013 2 comments

Have a Goal. In a broad sense, the goal defines the hero. A heroes goal could be to perform a sporting action at the highest level. Or to save lives in a medical emergency. Or rescue a cat from a tree. It’s in the heroes bloodline to attach themselves to a goal. Then, pursue it till completion. Work hard. Often we rely on a talent, a natural ability, that is effortless and easy to accomplish — something with zero gravity pushing against it. On an even playing field, this is okay — everyone is guilty of this, to some degree. But heroes have to remove their pride from the natural-abilities-talent equation, and push harder and faster. Over and over again, on repeat. This iteration prepares them for a moment when natural ability will not be sufficient capacity for the exercise. Persevere. If the goal defines the hero, then the journey qualifies them. And within its perimeter, there is a sufficient, and perhaps steady, “push” against a hero. It could be someone that provokes you, doubts you, inhibits you; it could be self-doubt, self-provocation, self-inhibition. This “push”-against-self isn’t fun or easy, but its challenge allows the required “pull” for you to learn, grow, overcome. The more you overcome — the more you “pull” — the more qualified the hero is along the course of their journey.React when needed. By definition, an Instant Hero evolves in the moment. It’s more active-reactive than any archetype of the Hero class because of its reliance on an instance. Whether it’s saving a child from a burning building or returning a wallet to a person after they dropped it, how you respond to a single moment will define you. Be passionate. Social Heroes are passionate about making a difference in society. They may be invested in the larger, more robust ideologues of social change (I.e. Marxism, the Civil Right’s Movement, etc.) or the smaller, more compact nuances of teaching at an elementary school. The mainline of every hero in this classification is their dedication to a specific passion involving human progression. Find a place to practice your passion. Employment is the next step, and it can be a difficult one. You know what you’re passionate about and you know which career type you want to pursue, but you can’t find a place to practice your skill on a daily basis. Or you can’t find gainful employment in the field. Relax. There’s a measure of uncertainty or disappointment associated with the Social Hero because so much of this hero is interconnected to a profession. If you can’t find a nonprofit or for-profit organization to work for, try this.
