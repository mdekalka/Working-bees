How to be a hero?

By piupiupiu September 25, 2013 2 comments

Have a Goal. In a broad sense, the goal defines the hero. A heroes goal could be to perform a sporting action at the highest level. Or to save lives in a medical emergency. Or rescue a cat from a tree. It’s in the heroes bloodline to attach themselves to a goal. Then, pursue it till completion. Work hard. Often we rely on a talent, a natural ability, that is effortless and easy to accomplish — something with zero gravity pushing against it. On an even playing field, this is okay — everyone is guilty of this, to some degree. But heroes have to remove their pride from the natural-abilities-talent equation, and push harder and faster. Over and over again, on repeat. This iteration prepares them for a moment when natural ability will not be sufficient capacity for the exercise.

Why do you have depression?

By piupiupiu September 26, 2013 0 comments

Depression can lead to emotional and physical problems. Typically, people with depression find it hard to go about their day-to-day activities, and may also feel that life is not worth living. Feeling sad, or what we may call "depressed", happens to all of us. The sensation usually passes after a while. However, people with a depressive disorder - clinical depression - find that their state interferes with daily life. Their normal functioning is undermined to such an extent that both they and those who care about them are affected by it. Depression is not uniform.
